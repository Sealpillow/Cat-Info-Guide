# Breed Index Improvement Roadmap

This document outlines potential enhancements for the Breed Index. The goal is **not to add significantly more written content**, but to make breed information easier to compare, discover, and understand while maintaining the existing museum/reference-book aesthetic.

---

# Design Philosophy

The Breed Index should remain:

- A curated reference catalogue.
- Easy to browse.
- Focused on factual information.
- Visually clean.
- Comparison-friendly.
- Consistent across every breed.

Avoid turning the guide into a blog or lengthy encyclopedia. Instead, prioritize scanability and usability.

---

# High Priority Improvements

### Purpose

One of the most common questions users have is:

> "Which breed is right for me?"

A comparison table allows users to evaluate multiple breeds at a glance without opening each profile.

- Shedding
- Intelligence
- Vocalness

# UX Improvements

## Better Visual Hierarchy

Prioritize information in this order:

1. Breed Name
2. Images
3. Key Facts
4. Lifestyle Badges
5. Personality Ratings
6. Breed Summary
7. Detailed History
8. Health
9. External References

Users should understand the breed within a few seconds before reading deeper.

---

## Increase Scanability

Whenever possible, replace long descriptive text with:

- Rating bars
- Chips
- Badges
- Icons
- Comparison tables

Reserve paragraphs for historical background and unique breed characteristics.

---

## Maintain Progressive Disclosure

Keep the existing card → modal interaction.

Cards should provide concise summaries.

Modals should contain the complete reference information.

Avoid placing all information directly on the card.

---

# Features Not Recommended

The following additions are unlikely to improve the guide and may detract from its reference-book identity:

- AI chatbot
- Breed recommendation quiz within the Breed Index (better as a separate tool)
- User comments or reviews
- Social media-style interactions
- Excessively long articles
- Video-heavy content

The strength of the Breed Index lies in being a concise, trustworthy catalogue rather than a content platform.

# Long-Term Vision

The Breed Index should feel like a modern digital field guide:

- Beautiful enough to browse casually.
- Practical enough to compare breeds before adoption.
- Structured enough to serve as a long-term reference.

Future enhancements should focus on improving **comparison, discoverability, and information hierarchy**, rather than increasing the amount of written content.
