Choking & CPR

What to do in the seconds before you reach a vet. Not a substitute for veterinary care — call one immediately either way.

Choking — Heimlich Maneuver

Most gagging isn't choking — hairballs, coughing & asthma look similar. True choking: silent panic, blue or pale gums, collapse.

1
Check the mouthLook for a visible object. Sweep it out with one finger — don't push blindly.
2
Abdominal thrustsHold upright against your chest. Thrust up under the ribcage, 3–5 times.
3
Recheck the mouthSweep out anything now visible.
4
Back blowsIf thrusts don't work: head angled down, firm strikes between the shoulder blades.
5
Get to a vet immediatelyEven after the object clears — injury from choking or the thrusts needs to be ruled out.

CPR — Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation

100–120/mincompression rate
⅓–½ chestcompression depth
30 : 2compress : breaths
Check for a few seconds: unconscious, not breathing, no heartbeat? If unsure, start CPR anyway.
1
Confirm before you startShake gently, call out to rule out sleep, then check breathing & heartbeat.
2
Clear the airwayTilt the head back, open the mouth, sweep for any obstruction.
3
Chest compressionsOn its side. Thumb over the heart, ⅓–½ chest depth, 100–120/min.
4
Rescue breathsEvery 30 compressions: close the mouth, breathe twice into the nose.
5
Repeat & reassess30:2, recheck every 2 minutes, until recovery or a vet takes over.
Alone? Skip CPR — go straight to the vet. It works best with two people: one compressing, one driving.
